How to pay

Alipay is the default everywhere in China (link Visa/MC) — taxis, street food, metro QR, tickets. WeChat Pay as fallback; foreign-card terminals are rare outside big hotels.

Deposits (押金): ¥100–500/night held at check-in at smaller hotels, refunded at checkout — that's the cash float.

Fees: card-funded Alipay carries your card's ~2–3% FX margin. A Wise-type card kills most of it.

Japan: Suica in Apple Wallet + some yen. HK: Octopus in Apple Wallet.

Apps & connectivity

Rides Didi (in Alipay) · Maps Amap 高德 / Apple Maps (Google Maps is dead in China, even on VPN) · Trains Trip.com (no VPN needed) · Metro Alipay "Transport" QR · Translate offline packs + Pleco.

VPN reality: Mullvad+Shadowsocks ≈ 50% on hotel WiFi — switch to eSIM data (routes out, no firewall) when it drops. Install the VPN before China; you can't download one inside.

Heat & typhoons

Heat: Chongqing / Xi'an / Shanghai run 33–40 °C with brutal humidity in July — outdoor mornings, indoor middays.

Typhoons: Jul 15–21 (Xiamen→Shenzhen→HK) is typhoon season. HSR pre-emptively suspends; HK raises T8 signals that pause flights. Zak's Jul 21 QF128 sits in this window.

Zhangjiajie: afternoon thunderstorms; cable cars pause in lightning — do the big mountain day on the best-weather morning of your three.
